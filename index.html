Energy Strategy
⚡ Your Daily Energy Blueprint
6:30–7:30am
Best gym window. Cortisol naturally peaks — you'll lift more, burn more, and it won't steal evening energy. Even 2–3 days/week of this changes everything.
Breakfast
Protein + slow carbs. Eggs + oats or Greek yogurt + banana. Skipping breakfast tanks afternoon energy and triggers evening overeating.
12:00–1:00pm
Lunch gym option if mornings don't work. Keeps evenings free for the kids. Eat 90 mins before — chicken + rice, not a heavy meal.
3:00–4:00pm
Energy dip window. Don't fight it with caffeine — a 10-min walk or 5-min stretching break resets you better than coffee after 2pm.
Evening
Protect this time. No high-intensity work after 7pm — it delays sleep. Light stretching (10 min) is allowed and actually helps recovery.
9:30pm
Sleep prep. Phone down. Blue light off. Protein snack (cottage cheese, casein shake) feeds overnight muscle repair. Aim for 7–8 hours.
Recovery by Session Type
⚽
After Football
  • Within 30 min: protein + carbs (banana + protein shake, or chicken wrap)
  • Ice calves and ankles if tender — 10 min each side
  • Light walk Tuesday if legs are heavy — don't sit all day
  • Foam roll quads and hamstrings Monday night, 5–8 mins
  • Epsom salt bath if seriously sore — genuinely helps
  • Tuesday gym: upper body only to let legs recover
🏋️
After Gym
  • 2-min machine stretch before leaving — don't skip this
  • Protein within 45 mins — 25–30g is the sweet spot
  • DOMS peaks Day 2 — movement (not rest) is the cure
  • Contrast shower: 1 min warm, 30 sec cold, repeat x3
  • Magnesium glycinate at night (200mg) — reduces soreness
  • Sleep 7+ hrs — that's when the actual muscle building happens
🏃
After Run / Active
  • 5-min walk cooldown — never stop dead from running
  • Calf raises on stairs for 2 min post-run to help foot recovery
  • Roll the plantar fascia with a golf ball (2 min each foot)
  • Elevate feet 15 mins if any swelling in the foot
  • Hydrate: at least 500ml water within 30 mins post-run
  • Monday rest day from running after weekend run
🦶
Foot Flare-Up Protocol
  • Stop immediately if sharp pain — don't push through foot pain
  • Swap run for: cycling (30 min), swimming (20 min), or 40-min brisk walk
  • Ice 15 min on the affected area, twice that day
  • Ibuprofen gel topically (not oral unless needed)
  • Avoid standing barefoot on hard floors — cushioned shoes all day
  • 3 consecutive flare-ups: see a physio — don't just manage it
🧠
Work Stress Management
  • Exercise IS a stress reliever — don't skip it on hard days, modify it
  • High stress = high cortisol = fat retention around the middle
  • 5-min box breathing before evening gym: 4 in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold
  • Treat your 30-min gym slot as a protected meeting in your diary
  • Busy week? Do the "Minimum Effective Dose" version (see each session card)
  • Missing one session never mattered. Missing a week is where habits break.
🥗
Nutrition Basics
  • Target: ~1,800–2,000 kcal/day on rest days, ~2,100 on training days
  • Protein: 140–160g/day — this protects muscle while in a deficit
  • Don't skip meals — energy crashes will kill evening motivation
  • Evening meal: protein + veg + small carbs. Not too heavy.
  • Alcohol: 2 units max on Fridays/Saturdays — it blocks fat burning for 24–36hrs
  • Hydration: 2.5L water minimum on training days
